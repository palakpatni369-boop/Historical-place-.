About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is admired worldwide for its exquisite architecture, symmetry, and intricate carvings, making it one of the most iconic monuments in the world.

It is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, on the southern bank of the Yamuna River. Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653, involving thousands of artisans, craftsmen, and laborers. The structure combines elements of Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles.

The Taj Mahal is historically important as it symbolizes eternal love and the grandeur of the Mughal Empire. It has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, attracting millions of visitors every year.

Interesting Facts

  • The Taj Mahal changes color depending on the sunlight and time of day.
  • It took over 20 years to complete construction.
  • Over 20,000 workers were involved in building it.
  • The white marble used was sourced from Makrana, Rajasthan.
  • The gardens are designed in a Persian-style Charbagh layout.
